01 Product context

SPT (short for Standard Parts Toolkit) bridges the gap between automotive parts data and Shopify storefronts. Six Shopify-app modules, three data sources, plus a separate Merchant of Record service.

i

A product of No Fixed Plans. ShowMeTheParts (operated by Vertical Development) is one of the supported parts-data sources — alongside ACES/PIES and CSV — not a partner or co-operator of SPT. Confirm exact wording with owner before publish.

Shopify-app modules

YMM Fitment Search

Year-Make-Model search with garage, configurable styling, and result behaviors.

VIN & License-Plate Lookup

Shoppers who don't know Y/M/M find fitting parts by VIN or plate (data-source dependent).

Catalog Parts Sync

Bulk product/variant sync incl. Description, Specs, Buyer's Guide, and Item Attributes.

PDP Enrichment

Fitment tables, attribute groups, notes, specs, and Buyer's Guide on product pages.

In-Cart Fitment & Fulfillment Handoff

Vehicle/fitment follows the shopper through cart and checkout, lands on the order for fulfillment.

Price & Inventory Sync

Inbound P&I via FTP or email attachments, on your cadence.

Order Export

Outbound delivery to Email, FTP, or HTTP API destinations.

Configuration Layer

Every module configurable for layout, styling, behavior, and data mapping.

Separate service: Merchant of Record

Distinct from the app

Sales-tax service

SPT acts as seller of record on your store: collects sales tax across US jurisdictions, remits to states through its own entity, and pays you consolidated monthly with a reconciliation report. Live today on the current site as its own service line.

Data sources

ACES / PIES XML

Industry-standard fitment + product info. The manufacturer path.

ShowMeTheParts API

API-driven fitment with 1,000+ supplier brands. The default for retailers.

Raw CSV

The fastest path for small or custom catalogs.

!

Positioning shift vs. the current site. The live standardpartstoolkit.com positions the product as a Shopify integration powered by ShowMeTheParts. The new site repositions as data-source agnostic and adds modules not on the current site (CSV, P&I sync, order export, multi-vehicle garage, multi-store).

Every capability beyond the current site must be operational at launch, or framed as "coming soon." Misalignment between marketing copy and shipping product is the biggest launch risk.

05 Brand direction

No existing assets — implementer proposes the full system.

Logo

Wordmark "SPT" + optional "SPT" monogram for favicon/app icon. SVG sources, light + dark variants.

Color system

Primary: industrial blue (~#1E3A8A). Accent: warm amber / safety-orange for CTAs. Neutrals: graphite/steel grey. Implement as Tailwind tokens mirrored to CSS variables so dark mode swaps cleanly.

Typography

Headings: Inter Tight / Space Grotesk / Manrope. Body: Inter at 16–17px. Mono: JetBrains Mono. Self-host via @fontsource.

Component conventions

Rounded corners rounded-xl. Soft elevation. Generous whitespace (py-20 sections). Solid primary / outlined secondary / ghost tertiary buttons.

Imagery

Annotated UI screenshots until real ones exist. Avoid stock automotive clichés (hands on steering wheels, generic engines). Prefer abstract data-viz, parts-diagram line art, or real UI.

Features overview /features/

Everything a parts store on Shopify needs, in one toolkit.

SPT covers the five jobs that turn a generic Shopify store into a real automotive parts store — fitment search, catalog sync, product detail enrichment, price/inventory ingestion, and order export. Use the whole toolkit or pick the modules you need.

YMM Fitment Search /features/ymm-search/
Fitment search
A fitment search shoppers actually finish.

Year, Make, Model — plus Sub-Model, Engine, and the other qualifiers your catalog needs. Configurable order, configurable styling, persistent garage. Built to be fast on storefronts with hundreds of thousands of SKUs.

How it works

  1. Connect your data — ACES/PIES, ShowMeTheParts, or CSV.
  2. Choose the qualifiers — pick which attributes appear as dropdowns and in what order.
  3. Drop the widget in — install on category pages, the home page, or any block your theme supports.

Three ways to find the right part

  • Year-Make-Model dropdowns — the standard. Predictable order, fast filtering, persistent garage.
  • VIN Lookup — shoppers who don't know Y/M/M paste a VIN and we resolve to the right vehicle (data-source dependent — ShowMeTheParts and most ACES/PIES vendor feeds do).
  • License-plate lookup — faster still for shoppers who only know the state and plate. Data-source dependent.

What you can configure

  • Dropdown order, labels, required vs optional qualifiers
  • Garage: save vehicles, restore on return, multi-vehicle support
  • VIN and license-plate entry: visible, toggle, or hidden
  • Layout: inline, modal, sidebar, sticky
  • Styling driven by your theme tokens
  • Result behavior: filter current collection, redirect, or both
  • "No fit" handling: hide, dim, or label

FAQ

  • How fast is it on a large catalog? The search index is precomputed at sync time, so dropdowns and filtering are constant-time regardless of catalog size.
  • Multi-vehicle garage? Yes. Persists across sessions for signed-in customers.
  • Works with my theme? Yes. Installs as a Shopify block; inherits theme tokens.
  • VIN decoding? Yes, when the data source provides it (ShowMeTheParts and ACES/PIES vendor feeds typically do; pure CSV does not).
Catalog Parts Sync /features/catalog-sync/
Catalog sync
Your authoritative catalog, faithfully reproduced in Shopify.

Push parts, variants, attributes, and fitment from the system of record into Shopify products and metafields — on a schedule, on demand, or driven by changes in the source.

How it works

  1. Point at the source — ACES/PIES file, ShowMeTheParts, or CSV.
  2. Map fields — choose how source attributes land in Shopify products, variants, metafields.
  3. Run it — full first sync, then incremental.

What you can configure

  • Full vs incremental sync; schedule + on-demand
  • Field mapping: any source attribute → any Shopify field/metafield
  • Conflict resolution: source-wins, Shopify-wins, per-field rules
  • Tagging and collection rules driven by attributes
  • Image handling: source URLs, hosted assets, or skip
  • Dry-run mode for previewing diffs

FAQ

  • First sync duration? A [100,000-SKU] catalog without image hosting completes in [under an hour]; with image hosting, bound by Shopify's upload throughput.
  • Will it overwrite manual edits? Only fields configured as source-wins.
  • SKU disappears from source? Configurable: archive, draft, mark out-of-stock, or no-op.
  • Multi-store / Shopify Plus? Yes. One source can drive multiple stores with per-store overrides.
PDP Product Information /features/pdp-enrichment/
Product detail pages
Every product page, fully informed. Fewer "didn't fit" returns.

Fitment tables, item attributes, specs, notes, and Buyer's Guide content rendered on the PDP — themed to match your store and driven by the same data that powers your YMM search. Real-time product data means shoppers see the most accurate fitment info, so they buy with confidence and you process fewer returns.

What you can configure

  • Which blocks appear, and in what order
  • Buyer's Guide block — render the manufacturer's buying guidance directly on the PDP
  • Product Item Attributes — structured attributes grouped per product type
  • Fitment table grouping (by vehicle, engine, collapsed-by-default)
  • Notes display: inline, footnoted, expandable
  • "Other vehicles this fits" cross-sell block
  • Show/hide based on whether shopper has selected a vehicle

The returns angle

Most "didn't fit" returns come from incomplete product data. PDP enrichment fixes that by rendering the manufacturer's authoritative fitment, attributes, and Buyer's Guide on every product page — kept current automatically because it reads from the same feed that powers your search.

In-Cart Fitment & Fulfillment Handoff /features/in-cart-fitment/ · NEW
In-cart fitment
The shopper's vehicle follows the order all the way to the warehouse.

The vehicle selected in YMM search travels with the shopper through the product page, the cart, and the checkout — and lands on the order so your fulfillment team sees it before they pick and pack. Ship the right part the first time.

How it works

  1. Capture at search. Vehicle picked in YMM search binds to the session.
  2. Persist through checkout. Stored as Shopify line-item properties + order metafields — visible in cart, on the order summary, and in the Shopify admin.
  3. Hand off to fulfillment. Team sees the vehicle on the order; optionally renders on the printed packing slip via a theme app extension.

What you can configure

  • Which fitment qualifiers travel with the order (vehicle only, or vehicle + engine + submodel + notes)
  • Whether the fitment record renders on the customer-facing order confirmation
  • Whether the fitment record renders on the printed packing slip
  • Whether shoppers can edit the captured vehicle from the cart
  • Whether the captured vehicle blocks checkout if the cart contains non-fitting items (or just warns)

FAQ

  • Works with standard Shopify checkout? Yes — uses line-item properties + order metafields. No checkout customization required.
  • Visible to my 3PL? Yes — Order Export carries the fitment record to any destination.
  • Can shoppers change their mind in cart? Configurable — edit in place, or require clearing the cart and re-searching.
Price & Inventory Sync /features/price-inventory-sync/
Price & inventory
Price and inventory updates from the channels your suppliers already use.

Drop a file on FTP. Send an email with an attachment. SPT picks it up, validates it, and updates Shopify on the cadence you choose.

What you can configure

  • Source: SFTP, FTP, or email attachment
  • Pickup schedule, or trigger-on-arrival for email
  • File format mapping (CSV, fixed-width, tab-delimited)
  • Multi-location inventory destination
  • Cost vs price vs compare-at price fields
  • Error reporting: email digest, webhook, or both
  • Thresholds for "too big a change" to apply automatically
Order Export /features/order-export/
Order export
Send orders to the warehouse, ERP, or 3PL the way they already expect them.

Push every paid order to Email, FTP/SFTP, or an HTTP API endpoint, formatted to match the system on the other side. Retries, deduplication, and audit trail included.

What you can configure

  • Destination: Email, FTP/SFTP, HTTP API
  • Format: CSV, XML, JSON, fixed-width
  • Trigger: order paid, fulfilled, tagged, channel-specific
  • Per-line-item routing across multiple destinations
  • Retry policy and backoff
  • Manual replay from run log

Merchant of Record
Sales tax, simplified.

Reduce sales tax headaches so you can focus on growing your automotive parts business. SPT becomes the seller of record on your store — we collect, remit, and report sales tax across US jurisdictions; you receive consolidated monthly payouts.

Book a Demo

What is a Merchant of Record?

Using a Merchant of Record service effectively outsources the transaction process. Your customer purchases from us; we pay you as the supplier, minus our fees. The entire customer experience stays branded as your company.

Three-step flow

  1. Collection. We facilitate checkout, calculating and collecting the necessary sales tax.
  2. Remittance. We remit the sales tax owed through our entity. Your business stays free of sales tax reporting and payment obligations.
  3. Payment. We send you order payouts, minus payment processing and service fees.

Benefits

Tracking & Reporting

We monitor sales-tax-rule changes and keep collection up to date. Reports are generated to match state requirements.

Simplicity

Post 1 · Understanding ACES and PIES for Shopify merchants draft 
---
title: "Understanding ACES and PIES for Shopify merchants"
description: "A plain-English guide to the two data standards behind automotive parts ecommerce — and what they mean if you sell on Shopify."
publishDate: 2026-05-15
author: "SPT"
tags: ["data", "aces-pies", "fundamentals"]
draft: false
---

If you sell automotive parts on Shopify, sooner or later you'll bump into two acronyms: ACES and PIES. They're the two data standards that quietly run the aftermarket parts industry. This post explains what each one is, why it exists, and what it means if your storefront lives on Shopify.

What is ACES? ACES — the Aftermarket Catalog Exchange Standard — is the answer to the question, "Which vehicles does this part fit?" Maintained by the AutoCare Association, ACES is an XML format that ties part numbers to vehicle applications using a controlled vocabulary (VCdb, PCdb). When a manufacturer publishes ACES, every line says, in plain English, "this part fits this engine in this submodel of this model in this year."

What is PIES? PIES — the Product Information Exchange Standard — answers "what is this part?" Where ACES describes fitment, PIES describes the part: descriptions, attributes, packaging, hazmat flags, digital assets, marketing copy. Designed to be used together.

Why it matters on Shopify. Shopify wasn't built for the aftermarket. It has products, variants, metafields. It does not natively understand "this brake pad fits the 2018–2022 F-150 with the 6.2L engine." Bridging that gap is exactly the gap SPT closes.

If you don't have ACES/PIES. Most retailers don't. ShowMeTheParts gives you API-driven fitment without maintaining files, and a clean CSV will get you a working fitment search faster than most teams expect.

Post 2 · Why YMM search is the difference between a parts store and a Shopify store draft 
---
title: "Why YMM search is the difference between a parts store and a Shopify store"
description: "Year-Make-Model search isn't a nice-to-have for automotive parts — it's the only way most shoppers know how to find the right product."
publishDate: 2026-05-01
author: "SPT"
tags: ["ymm", "ux", "fundamentals"]
draft: false
---

A shopper walks into your Shopify store looking for a serpentine belt. They don't know the part number. They don't know the OEM cross-reference. They know it's for a 2014 Silverado 1500 with the 5.3L V8. If your store can't take that input and return only the parts that fit, you've already lost them.

The "fits what" problem. General-purpose ecommerce search is built around words: titles, descriptions, tags. Parts ecommerce is built around relationships. No keyword search will reliably narrow "brake pads" to "brake pads for the front of a 2014 Silverado 1500 6-lug." You need a structured filter driven by real fitment data.

What good YMM looks like: predictable order, persistent garage, honest "no fit" handling, sub-quarter-second dropdown populating.

What gets it wrong: forcing vehicle selection before browsing, showing non-fitting products after a vehicle is selected, resetting the garage on navigation.

YMM search isn't a feature on a parts store; it's the floor. Get it right and the rest of the catalog experience gets easier.
